When you convert a file on a typical “upload and convert” site, your file is sent to their servers. Even if they don’t store it long-term, it’s on their infrastructure long enough to be logged, scanned, or mishandled. For anything personal or sensitive, that’s a privacy risk you can avoid.

**Local conversion** means the conversion runs on your machine—in your browser—using code that you load once (for example FFmpeg compiled to WebAssembly). Your file is read in the tab, processed there, and you download the result. No upload, no server in the loop. That gives you control over where your data goes and often feels faster for single files because there’s no upload delay.

We built PrivacyConvert around this idea: conversion in the browser, no upload, zero need to trust a server with your files. Your files never leave your device.
