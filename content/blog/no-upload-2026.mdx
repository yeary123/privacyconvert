Why does no-upload, client-side conversion matter? When you convert a file on a typical online converter, the file is sent to their servers. That means someone else can store, log, or inspect it. For personal documents, photos, or anything sensitive, that’s a real privacy risk.

**No-upload converters** run the conversion entirely in your browser. Your file never leaves your device. The converter code (for example FFmpeg compiled to WebAssembly) runs in the tab; you get the result back without any upload. That’s better for privacy and often faster for small batches, since there’s no round-trip to a server.

How do you choose? Prefer tools that clearly state that conversion is client-side or in-browser, and avoid ones that require you to “upload” or “send” the file. Check whether they load a large script or WASM once (that’s the converter engine) versus streaming your file out.
