WebP is common on the web and in mobile exports, but many desktop apps and older workflows still expect PNG. Converting WebP to PNG lets you open and edit files in those tools without changing how you work.

Doing that conversion in the **browser** keeps your files off anyone else’s servers. You open the converter page, choose your WebP file, and get a PNG back. The conversion runs locally in the tab; nothing is uploaded. That’s useful for screenshots, exported graphics, or any WebP you’d rather have as PNG for compatibility or editing.
