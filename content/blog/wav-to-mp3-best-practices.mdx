## Why Convert WAV to MP3?

- **Size.** WAV files are large (e.g. 10 MB per minute at 44.1 kHz stereo). MP3 at 192–320 kbps can be 10x smaller with minimal audible loss for most uses.
- **Compatibility.** MP3 plays everywhere: phones, cars, streaming, and legacy devices. WAV is not always supported.
- **Sharing and storage.** Email, messaging, and cloud storage work better with smaller MP3s. Converting locally keeps the process **browser local** and **no upload 2026**.

Doing this with a **privacy first file converter** means your audio is never sent to a server—ideal for voice memos, interviews, or any sensitive recording.

## Best Practices for WAV to MP3

### 1. Choose the Right Bitrate

- **Voice / speech:** 128 kbps is often enough.
- **Music, podcasts, general use:** 192–256 kbps VBR is a good balance.
- **Archiving / high quality:** 256–320 kbps or VBR equivalent.

PrivacyConvert’s [WAV to MP3](/convert/wav-to-mp3) tool uses sensible defaults (e.g. VBR quality 2); you get high quality with **completely local** conversion.
