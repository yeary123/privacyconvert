## What is “invisible text”?

**Invisible text** means Unicode characters that are valid in text but have no visible glyph. The most common are:

- **Zero Width Space** (U+200B) — often used to make an “empty” name or to break lines without a visible space.
- **Zero Width Non-Joiner** (U+200C) — used in some scripts; also used as invisible filler.
- **Zero Width Joiner** (U+200D) — used in emoji/script joining; Emojipedia and similar sites document these.
- **Word Joiner** (U+2060) — invisible, doesn’t break lines (useful in URLs or code).

Sites like **Emojipedia** document these in the context of emoji and Unicode. When you search **“emojipedia invisible text”**, you’re often looking for a quick way to **copy** these characters.
