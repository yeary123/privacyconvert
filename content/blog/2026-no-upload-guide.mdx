In 2026, more users care about where their files go. **No-upload converters** run entirely in your browser: your PDF, images, and media never leave your device.

## Why no upload?

- **Privacy** — Cloud converters (e.g. Convertio) upload your file to their servers. No-upload tools keep everything local.
- **Speed** — No upload means no wait for a round-trip; conversion starts as soon as the file is selected.
- **Control** — You decide what runs (e.g. FFmpeg in the browser) and what data is exposed.

## How it works

Tools like PrivacyConvert load **FFmpeg compiled to WebAssembly** (or similar runtimes) in the tab. Your file is read by the page, processed in memory, and the result is offered for download. No server ever sees the bytes.

## Choosing a tool in 2026

Look for:

1. **Client-side only** — No “uploading…” for the actual conversion.
2. **Clear limits** — Free vs Pro (batch, history) so you know what you get.
3. **Open stack** — FFmpeg.wasm, Canvas, WebCodecs: tech you can verify.

PrivacyConvert follows this model: free single-file conversion for most formats, Pro for batch and P2P. All processing stays in your browser — no upload 2026.
