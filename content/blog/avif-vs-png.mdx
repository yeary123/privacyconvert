AVIF and PNG both have clear strengths. **AVIF** gives much smaller file sizes at similar visual quality, so it’s great for photos and web assets where bandwidth matters. **PNG** is lossless and widely supported everywhere—editors, older apps, and every browser. PNG also supports transparency without the compatibility quirks of AVIF in some tools.

When should you convert AVIF to PNG? Use PNG when you need maximum compatibility (e.g. sending to someone who might open it in an old app), when you’re editing in software that doesn’t handle AVIF well, or when you want a single, lossless frame from an AVIF image. The other way around, convert PNG to AVIF when you’re optimizing for the web and want smaller files without losing too much quality.
