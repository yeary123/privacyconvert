## Why Batch Conversion?

- **Efficiency.** Convert dozens or hundreds of AVIF, WebP, WAV, or other files in one go instead of one by one.
- **Consistency.** Same settings and same tool for every file.
- **Workflow.** Export from a camera or recorder, then batch convert to MP3 or PNG locally with a **no upload** tool.

When batch runs **client side** and **browser local**, you get that efficiency without sending files to a server—**no upload 2026**.

## Why Keep Batch Local?

Cloud batch converters (e.g. Convertio) typically require you to upload all files. That means:

- **Privacy risk.** Every file exists on their servers. For personal or sensitive content, that’s a problem.
- **Compliance.** Regulated or internal data often can’t be uploaded to third-party services. **Completely local** batch avoids that.
- **Control.** With a **privacy first file converter**, you never depend on their retention or deletion policies.

Local batch with **no upload 2026** keeps every file on your device from start to finish.

## Best Practices for Local Batch Conversion

- **Use a modern browser.** WebAssembly and enough memory help. Close other tabs if you have many or large files.
- **Reasonable batch size.** Hundreds of small files are fine; for very large files (e.g. long WAVs), smaller batches may be more stable.
- **Keep originals.** Batch convert to a new folder so you still have the source files.
- **Pick the right format.** Use [WAV to MP3](/convert/wav-to-mp3) for audio, [AVIF to PNG](/convert/avif-to-png) or [WebP to PNG](/convert/webp-to-png) for images—all **privacy first file converter** tools, **completely local**.
