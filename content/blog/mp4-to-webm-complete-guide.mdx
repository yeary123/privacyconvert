## Why Convert MP4 to WebM?

- **Web performance.** WebM (VP9) often delivers smaller file sizes at similar quality, so pages load faster.
- **Royalty-free.** VP9 and Opus avoid licensing concerns that come with H.264 in some regions.
- **HTML5 compatibility.** Modern browsers support WebM natively for &lt;video&gt; embedding.
- **Consistency.** One format for web delivery simplifies your pipeline.

Doing this conversion **client side** and **browser local** keeps your footage on your device—**no upload 2026**, so no privacy or compliance risk.

## What Is WebM?

WebM is an open, royalty-free container format using VP9 for video and Opus (or Vorbis) for audio. It was designed for web use and is supported in Chrome, Firefox, Edge, and other browsers. Converting MP4 to WebM locally means you never upload video to a third party.
