## Why Convert AVIF to PNG?

- **Compatibility.** Many older apps, design tools, and platforms still work best with PNG. Converting to PNG avoids “unsupported format” issues.
- **Editing.** Some editors don’t support AVIF yet. PNG is widely supported for layers, transparency, and lossless edits.
- **Transparency.** AVIF supports alpha; PNG does too and is often better supported in workflows that need transparency.
- **Archiving.** PNG is a stable, lossless format that’s easy to archive and open anywhere.

Doing this conversion **client side** and **browser local** keeps your images on your device—**no upload 2026**, so no privacy or compliance trade-off.

## What Is AVIF?

AVIF (AV1 Image File Format) is a modern image format based on the AV1 video codec. It typically delivers much smaller files than JPEG or PNG at similar quality, and supports transparency and HDR. Browsers and an increasing number of apps support it, but PNG remains the universal fallback for compatibility and editing.

## What Is PNG?

PNG (Portable Network Graphics) is a lossless raster format with broad support for transparency and wide tooling. It’s the go-to when you need lossless quality and maximum compatibility. Converting AVIF to PNG gives you a safe, portable version of your image.
